<h1 dir="ltr">Is Your Child's Learning App Actually Safe? 5 Red Flags to Watch for in 2026</h1><p dir="ltr"><img src="https://instasize.com/api/image/76f1622a7962e1791319017aec4b5345cfbd4b06c2a66ba7b736bf3e714d9f03.png" width="909" height="773"></p><p dir="ltr">"Safe" for a kids' app used to just mean no violence and no scary content. In 2026, it means something much bigger: what data is being collected, who it's shared with, and whether the company is even legally allowed to have it. Here's how to actually check if your child's learning app is safe, red flag by red flag.</p><h2 dir="ltr"><strong>5 Red Flags to Watch for in 2026&nbsp;</strong></h2><h3 dir="ltr"><strong>Red Flag #1: Vague or Bundled Consent Screens</strong></h3><p dir="ltr">Under the<a href="https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/topics/protecting-consumer-privacy-security/kids-privacy-coppa"> <strong>FTC's updated COPPA rule</strong></a><strong>,</strong> apps are now required to ask for separate permission before sharing a child's data with third parties. If an app rolls everything &mdash; account setup, ads, data sharing &mdash; into one blanket "I agree" screen, that's a clear kids app privacy red flag. Transparent apps break consent into distinct, readable steps.</p><h3 dir="ltr"><strong>Red Flag #2: No Clear Data Retention Policy</strong></h3><p dir="ltr">Per the<a href="https://www.freshfields.com/en/our-thinking/blogs/a-fresh-take/a-new-era-for-child-online-privacy-updated-coppa-rule-and-maryland-kids-code-imp-102mop1"> <strong>2026 COPPA amendments</strong></a><strong>,</strong> companies are now required to publish a written data retention policy and delete a child's data once it's no longer needed for its original purpose &mdash; a class assignment finished, a subscription cancelled. If you can't find any statement about how long your child's data is kept, assume the worst.</p><h3 dir="ltr"><strong>Red Flag #3: Unnecessary Permissions (Camera, Mic, Location)</strong></h3><p dir="ltr">A learning app has no legitimate reason to request a child's precise location or continuous microphone access unless that's core to a feature you actually use. Check what permissions an app has requested &mdash; on iOS: Settings &gt; Privacy &amp; Security; on Android: Settings &gt; Apps &gt; Permissions &mdash; and revoke anything the app doesn't need to function.</p><h3 dir="ltr"><strong>Red Flag #4: Biometric Data Collection</strong></h3><p dir="ltr">This is new territory in 2026. The<strong><a href="https://stateofsurveillance.org/news/coppa-2026-new-rules-children-privacy-biometric-data/"> updated COPPA rule</a> </strong>has expanded the definition of protected children's data to include biometric identifiers like face scans, voiceprints, and even movement patterns picked up by motion-based games. If an app uses face filters, voice avatars, or fingerprint logins for a young child, ask exactly why &mdash; and whether that data can be deleted on request.</p><h3 dir="ltr"><strong>Red Flag #5: No Independent Safety Certification</strong></h3><p dir="ltr">Reputable kids learning apps are often reviewed by<a href="https://privacy.commonsense.org/"> <strong>Common Sense Media's privacy program</strong></a><strong>, </strong>which verifies COPPA compliance and content quality independently, rather than relying on the company's own claims. If an app can't point to any third-party verification and dodges direct questions about its privacy practices, treat that evasiveness itself as the red flag.</p><p dir="ltr"><img src="https://instasize.com/api/image/cf77cca9c582b20b2c2ab8ce8585408fd49512e1fe52babc79d41ae123cd5441.png" width="571" height="812"></p><h2 dir="ltr"><strong>The Simple Filter</strong></h2><p dir="ltr">Ask three questions before your child opens any new app:</p><ol><li dir="ltr" aria-level="1"><p dir="ltr" role="presentation">Can I find a plain-language privacy policy in under two minutes?</p></li><li dir="ltr" aria-level="1"><p dir="ltr" role="presentation">Does it ask for only the permissions it clearly needs?</p></li><li dir="ltr" aria-level="1"><p dir="ltr" role="presentation">Is there an actual named company and support contact behind it &mdash;
